Want To Know About Me? Read-

2011 - Present

Life is a Rollercoaster of ups and downs, but too bad I haven’t been to one just yet! I’m 12, so they don’t let me ride the amazing ride; It’s annoying.

Meet me, Fatiha Sharin Samiha. Did you know that my name spelled backward is Ahitaf Nirahs? It’s a really cool name, and I like it. It is just as cool as learning what happened to the Third Reich in the second world war 50 years ago. Or cool like knowing that the bluer and whiter the stars are, the hotter they are instead of colder as others’ initial guess. Don’t worry, I have been there. It’s the same as being fooled by the names of Iceland and Greenland. Cause I’ll tell you, Iceland’s got volcanoes in it while Greenland is a complete island equivalent to Siberia. Also, India and Bangladesh are not the same. Stop saying so.

I was born in 2011, a year when the last space shuttle mission was conducted and… Osama Bin Laden was killed. Wow. And, have you heard the song “Rolling in the Deep” by Adele? This song was also released in 2011, just like me. Wow again. But there’s something more to my story. I was born in Bangladesh, a land of vibrant culture, rich history, and breathtaking natural beauty which is just not about India. While I may have a global perspective, my roots are firmly planted in this not-so-hot and not-so-cool country. Yes, Bangladesh is a country of neutral weather!

You know, Biryani isn’t just from India. It’s from the Muslims of south-east Asia. I love Biryani way too much. Royal Bengal Tiger is Bengal, from Bangladesh, not Indian (I’ve heard people from the internet say it’s Indian). It really pisses me off when people recognize stuff from Bangladesh as Indian.

My family consists of my father, my mother, and my annoying younger brother who I still love a lot. Damn you Tonoy, I hate you, but I love you, but I hate you, yet I keep sympathizing with you. Just grow up already, bro.

I started studying at Monipur High School (and College!), a prestigious school in our country located in Dhaka, the capital city of Bangladesh, when I was 7 years old. I currently study in the English Version of the school in class 7. It may seem it has been 7 years, but no, not really; it has been 6 years. I skipped class 4; shh. Also, there was the COVID-19 pandemic, I didn’t need to go to school for almost 2 years!

I’ve won quizzes from my school, although it was mainly science. I also won once in a history quiz!

Currently, I'm participating in the English Olympiads’ final round. Let's see if I can secure a high place.

I started learning programming at the age of 10 from the institution of Bright Champs. I learned Python, C, C++, HTML/CSS/JS, Thunkable programming, as well as Scratch, and a little bit of Java. Why did I not learn more of Java when it’s so popular? Have you seen the size of that monstrosity when just printing ‘Hello, world!’?! I ain’t wasting that much of my time, heck no. But I might later on. Cheer for me, my fellow Homo sapiens, the pinnacle of the animal kingdom and the possessors of unparalleled intellectual capabilities. Still, I bet you that we all are becoming dumber each day because of the internet.

And, I learned about SQL, PHP, and a lot more about C through CS50, so shout out to you guys!

I mostly work with web pages, it’s kind of like a passion, a coping mechanism from my surroundings. I’m trying to learn more about JavaScript and its frameworks and libraries, same with CSS. Python is my second favorite programming language after HTML/CSS/JS pact, although I know these are not true programming languages (except for JS). It’s way better than Java, and you can almost do everything that you could in Java, but the syntax is way simpler and shorter.

An interesting thing about me is that I’m one of the weirdest people I know. I act like an introvert but surely no introvert will go, walk up to people just to ask, “Who do you think would be the closest to Syria?” and interview them (The answers to the “Syria’s friend” question really gave so many weird answers, at least for me. HOW WILL SERBIA OR SIBERIA BE RELATED? SINGAPORE? REALLY FELLAS? Most people said Russia, which, you know, makes sense. But still, it’s Lebanon and no one even remembered that beautiful piece of territory!).

Did you know I change my preferences almost every second? Mostly yearly though. I remember I hated history so much, but this year, I’m in love with this piece of personality. I learned Japanese and was in love with Japan, but mainly because of anime and its technology. After learning about Japanese history, I gave up instantly. I’m really mad at Japan. Sure you need to move on, but changing your whole self is a new thing. Also, Kanji is too hard for me.

I went to learn Spanish after that and gave up almost instantly. Why? Because I wasn’t actually planning to live there! Yes, yes, I know Spain is beautiful and their ‘siestas’ are amazing (¡Me encanta dormir!). But I really don’t plan on staying there and living my life there.

…Now I’m studying Russian. Learning Russian gives me access to all of the former Soviet republics, and I love the stan countries. I love love the stan countries.

People always say Russian is way too hard, which isn’t true. If you actually pay attention to the details, it’s as easy as English! I think that’s the same with Chinese and Arabic, and every language. But for me, it’s Russian :).

After everything, we are all Homo sapiens, the pinnacle of the animal kingdom and the possessors of unparalleled intellectual capabilities (unless there’s a Martian or a Titanian or something else). We all probably have different opinions, sure. I may be different from you, I may think differently from you, and all. But let us unite and make the world a better place (and maybe stop climate change as well!). Remember, ‘Alone we fall, united we stand.’ Peace, mates ✌🏻.
